Multiplicity

An interactive installation exploring the boundaries between physical and digital space through real-time projection mapping and motion tracking.

2019 · Featured Installation Interactive
16 : 10 image placeholder

Multiplicity is an immersive installation that transforms architectural surfaces into living, breathing canvases. Using an array of depth sensors and custom software built in openFrameworks, the work responds to the presence and movement of visitors in real time — creating a dialogue between body, light, and space.

Concept

The piece draws on ideas from quantum mechanics — specifically the superposition principle — where particles exist in multiple states simultaneously until observed. In Multiplicity, the projected imagery holds latent patterns that only resolve into coherent forms when a viewer enters the space. Each visitor collapses the visual field differently, meaning no two experiences are identical.

The aesthetic language is deliberately restrained: monochromatic fields of slowly morphing geometry, punctuated by moments of sudden crystallisation when the system detects stillness. This interplay between motion and pause, chaos and order, mirrors the tension at the heart of the quantum metaphor.

Technical Approach

The installation runs on a cluster of four machines, synchronised via a lightweight OSC protocol. Three Intel RealSense D455 cameras provide overlapping depth maps that are fused into a single point-cloud representation of the room. Custom GLSL shaders translate this volumetric data into a continuous 2D projection surface, maintaining the spatial coherence visitors expect while introducing uncanny distortions at the edges of perception.

Software Stack

Built with openFrameworks (C++), the rendering pipeline uses a deferred-shading approach to handle the high polygon count of the point cloud without sacrificing frame rate. Key libraries include:

  • ofxRealSense2 — depth camera integration
  • ofxOsc — inter-machine communication
  • ofxGui — real-time parameter tuning
  • Custom Voronoi tessellation solver for the fragmentation visuals

"The most compelling interactive works are those where the technology becomes invisible — where the visitor forgets they are triggering changes and simply inhabits a world that seems to breathe alongside them."

Exhibition Context

First shown at Kunsthal Charlottenborg in Copenhagen as part of the New Senses group exhibition (2019), Multiplicity occupied a 12 × 8 metre darkened gallery space. The work was subsequently presented at Ars Electronica (Linz, 2020) and ZKM (Karlsruhe, 2021), each time adapted to the specific dimensions and acoustics of the host venue.

The piece is designed to accommodate anywhere from one to thirty simultaneous visitors. In smaller groups the experience is meditative and personal; as the crowd grows, the projections become increasingly turbulent and unpredictable, shifting the emotional register from contemplation to collective energy.

Materials & Equipment

Custom software (openFrameworks / C++), 3× Intel RealSense D455, 4× projection-mapped surfaces, 4-channel spatial audio
